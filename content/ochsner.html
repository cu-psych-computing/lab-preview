The Social, Cognitive, and Affective Neuroscience Lab

The Social, Cognitive, and Affective Neuroscience Lab is not currently seeking research assistants

Research Projects

Research in the Social, Cognitive and Affective Neuroscience lab examines the psychological and neural processes involved in extracting social, emotional, and cognitive meaning from the world.

As a research assistant, your involvement may include gathering relevant articles, recruiting participants, running studies, and assisting in data analysis. The requested time commitment is about 10-15 hours per week.

Some current projects in our lab investigate: emotion experience and regulation in young versus elderly participants, emotion and self-regulation in individuals suffering from depression and suicidal tendencies, self-regulation of alcohol cravings in problem drinkers, the long-term effects of emotion regulation training, internalized stigma of those who are at clinical high-risk for developing psychosis, and more! We collect behavioral, psychophysiological and neural (fMRI) measures.
